Creating Your Monoprints Homework

Monoprint Process: 4 Simple Steps

1
Color the Tracing Paper
  • Take tracing paper
  • Cover one side with black oil pastel
2
Add Objects for Texture
  • Place relevant stencil materials related to your project on paper
  • Put your tracing paper on top (pastel side down)
3
Rub to Transfer
  • Use a pencil to rub over the paper
  • Press firmly to transfer the color
  • Make sure to cover all areas
4
Reveal Your Print
  • Carefully lift the tracing paper
  • Remove the objects
  • Your monoprint is now on the plain paper

Troubleshooting Tips Homework

If Your Print is Too Light
  • Apply more oil pastel to the tracing paper
  • Press harder when rubbing with pencil
  • Try using a spoon for more even pressure
Paper Issues
  • Use smooth white paper for best results
  • Make sure tracing paper is thin enough
  • Tape paper down to prevent shifting

PART 2: IN-CLASS - Digitize and Develop Your Monoprints

Class Activities

Today you'll learn to digitize and enhance your monoprints for your Term 2 project

1. Digitize (30 mins)

  • Scan or import your monoprints
  • Save files in appropriate format
  • Create backup copies

2. Process (45 mins)

  • Apply Threshold adjustment
  • Remove white areas
  • Create transparent backgrounds

3. Develop (45 mins)

  • Add layer masks
  • Experiment with blend modes
  • Incorporate into your Term 2 project

Materials Needed for Class

For Digital Work:

  • Your completed monoprints (physical or scanned)
  • Access to scanner (if not already scanned)
  • Computer with Photoshop installed
  • USB drive for backup (optional)
Key Photoshop Terms You'll Use Today:
  • Threshold: Converts images to pure black and white (no grays)
  • Layer Mask: Hides parts of an image without deleting them
  • Blend Modes: Different ways layers interact with each other
  • Magic Wand: Selection tool that selects similar colored areas

Step 1: Digitizing Your Prints In-Class

Scanning Tips
  1. Set scanner to 300dpi or higher
  2. Scan as a color image (even if black/white)
  3. Make sure the print lies flat on the scanner
No Scanner? Use a phone camera in good lighting, hold camera directly above the print.
Photoshop Setup
  1. Open Photoshop and create a new A3 landscape document (300dpi)
  2. Import your scanned image (File → Place Embedded)
  3. Save a copy as PSD to preserve all layers

Step 2: Digital Processing In-Class

Processing Your Print in Photoshop

1
Apply Threshold

First, convert your image to pure black and white:

  1. Go to Image → Adjustments → Threshold
  2. Move the slider until you like the contrast
  3. Click OK to apply
2
Select White Areas

Next, select the white background to remove it:

  1. Use the Rectangular Marquee Tool (M)
  2. Draw a selection around a white area
  3. Go to Select → Similar
3
Remove White Areas

Create transparency by removing the white:

  1. With white areas selected, press Delete
  2. This removes white, leaving only your texture
  3. If needed, repeat for any remaining white areas

Step 3: Creative Development In-Class

Enhancing Your Texture

1
Add a Layer Mask

Use a mask to control which parts of the texture appear:

  1. Click the "Add Layer Mask" button in Layers panel
  2. Use black brush to hide parts of the texture
  3. Use white brush to reveal parts again
2
Try Different Blend Modes

Experiment with how your texture interacts with backgrounds:

  1. In the Layers panel, try these blend modes:
    • Multiply: Darkens underlying layers
    • Overlay: Adds contrast while keeping details
    • Screen: Lightens underlying layers
  2. Adjust layer opacity to control intensity
3
Incorporate Into Your Project

Add your texture to your Term 2 project:

  1. Copy your processed texture to your project file
  2. Position and scale as needed
  3. Experiment with colors and effects
Tip: Try applying Image → Adjustments → Hue/Saturation to add color to your black texture.
